Frequently Asked Questions

Have a question about tattooing?
Why not hear from the professionals:


Are Tattoos Safe?

Yes tattoos are safe, providing the tattoo is carried out by a professional in a sterile environment. A professional tattooer will undertake many levels of precaution and practice to ensure that it is applied in a safe and sterile manner. This is why home tattooing is so dangerous.....just because the needle comes out of a packet doesn't mean it’s a safe procedure. The next step is down to the customer to keep the tattoo clean whilst healing. So providing each step of tattooing is carried out correctly, yes tattooing is safe.


Does It Hurt?

It sure does! Anyone who says different is trying to look cool, but at the same time lying. It’s needles penetrating your skin, say no more. But it can’t be that bad, otherwise no one would have them!


How Much Does It Cost?

A good tattoo will not be cheap, simple. If someone is offering discount, there is probably a reason why. Price shopping for tattoos is a huge mistake. A professional tattooer will charge a fair price for the work involved and the worth of the final product.


How Long Do They Take To Heal?

That depends on the tattoo and the person healing it. A well executed tattoo will heal smooth and fast. A healthy person will heal a tattoo a lot quicker too. Healing times can vary from 4-14 days.


Should I Tip My Tattooist?

Yes! We put our heart and soul into creating something you will have forever, a tip is a very welcome thank you.


What Makes Tattoos Fade?

Faded tattoos can be because of poor execution, poor healing or exposure to direct sunlight.


What Aftercare Is Best For A New Tattoo?

There are many methods of aftercare. Your tattooer will recommend what they thinks work best for their style of tattooing. If they don’t tell you, ask!


When Is The Best Time Of Year To Get A Tattoo?

Any time of year is fine, providing you are willing to heal the tattoo properly. So if you choose to get a tattoo in mid summer, don’t expect to be sunbathing for a few weeks! We find a lot of experienced collectors prefer the winter months to get tattooed.


How Do I Choose A Tattooist?

Do your research, ensure the person you choose is capable of the style and design you want. Meet the tattooer and talk with them about your tattoo, don’t just send a message asking ‘how much for this one’. We want to provide a great tattoo and experience. Ask to see their work, make sure you feel comfortable in their shop and with them.


How Do I Choose A Tattoo?

Do not follow fashion! Would you still wear clothes you had 5 years ago? Well the same applies to tattoos, they will last a lot longer than the pair of shoes that were hip last week! Work with a tattooer to create something you will wear forever.


Should I Design My Own Tattoo?

Some design input is always welcome, but please let the tattooer do their job, they know what they are talking about. So if they say something won’t work or would look better a different way, they are saying it for your benefit.
